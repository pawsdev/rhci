Emergency Notification Sign/USDOT Number Photo

If the number is unreadable, check the other approach to see if the number is better displayed.

USDOT NUMBER PHOTO

If there is no ENS sign or the crossing number is not shown on the sign, capture a photo of the crossing number displayed on the railroad equipment

Roadway Direction 1

If safe, Photos should be taken from the travel lanes. On busy roadways, Photos can be taken from the edge of pavement and from the median. One person should watch for traffic while the other person takes the photos. Photos should encompass the entire gate and/or cantilever that is nearest the approach lanes.

Take a photo of the crossing from one side of the approaching roadway.

All warning devices immediately adjacent to the crossing (i.e. signals, lights, gates, signs) should appear in the photo.

Roadway Direction 2

If safe, Photos should be taken from the travel lanes. On busy roadways, Photos can be taken from the edge of pavement and from the median. One person should watch for traffic while the other person takes the photos. Photos should encompass the entire gate and/or cantilever that is nearest the approach lanes.

Take a photo of the crossing from one side of the approaching roadway.

All warning devices immediately adjacent to the crossing (i.e. signals, lights, gates, signs) should appear in the photo.

LANDSCAPE

Roadway Direction 1 Supplemental


If a crossing has more than two lanes in one direction or has a median,then two photos should be taken on each side. If a side walk cannot be clearly seen in a photo,then an extra photo should be taken from the sidewalk at the same distance from the crossing as the first photo.

If needed,take an additional photo of the crossing from Direction 2 to capture warning devices that do not appear in the first photo(i.e. When is to wide). Supplemental photos are required for roads with more than two lanes is one direction or if road has a median.
